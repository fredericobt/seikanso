We live in an age of distraction. Every device competes for a slice of our awareness. The result is a kind of fragmented living — present everywhere, nowhere fully.

Attention is not a passive act. It is a choice, renewed moment to moment.

## What Deserves Your Gaze

Not everything. That's the point. The practice of attention is also the practice of discernment — learning what is worth the gift of your focus.

A conversation with a friend. A sentence that stops you cold. The quality of afternoon light.

## The Work

When you sit down to write, or to build, or to think — give it everything. Not the split-screen version of yourself, but the whole thing.

The work will know the difference.
