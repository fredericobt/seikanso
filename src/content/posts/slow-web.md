The fast web is exhausting. Infinite scroll, push notifications, engagement metrics — a system optimized for capture, not meaning.

The slow web is something different.

## What It Looks Like

A personal site that hasn't been redesigned in three years. A newsletter that arrives once a month. A blog post that takes its time.

The slow web isn't slow because it's lazy. It's slow because it respects your time.

## Building for Slowness

When I built this site, I wanted it to feel quiet. No trackers. No pop-ups. No "subscribe to my newsletter" interrupting the reading.

Just words, space, and a way to get in touch if something resonates.

That's enough.
