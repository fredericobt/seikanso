The first hour of the day is the most valuable. Before the inbox, before the news, before anyone else's priorities arrive.

I've been protecting mine.

## What Slow Looks Like

No phone for the first thirty minutes. Coffee made without distraction. A few sentences written by hand before touching a keyboard.

It sounds small. It changes everything.

## The Compound Effect

A single slow morning does little. But a hundred of them builds a different kind of relationship with your own thinking. You start the day already in conversation with yourself, rather than in reaction to the world.

The work that comes out of slow mornings is different work. More considered. More yours.
