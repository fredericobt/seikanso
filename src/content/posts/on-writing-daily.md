The first week is the hardest. You sit down, nothing comes, and you wonder why you're doing this at all.

Then something shifts.

## Momentum

Writing daily is less about producing great work and more about staying in relationship with language. You keep the channel open. When something worth saying arrives, you're ready.

Most daily writing is bad. That's fine. It's the practice that matters, not the output.

## What I've Learned

You write better after writing badly for a while. The bad sentences clear the way for the better ones.

Show up. Write the bad sentences. Wait.
