There is a temptation to measure reading by volume. Books finished, pages turned, titles accumulated.

But the most useful reading I've done has always been slow. A paragraph re-read three times. A margin note that turns into a whole idea. A book returned to years later with new eyes.

## Quality Over Quantity

Speed-reading strips the pleasure from reading the same way fast food strips nutrition from eating. You get the calories, not the nourishment.

Some books deserve weeks. A few deserve years.

## What Stays

After finishing a book, ask yourself: what will you remember in a month? In a year? If the answer is "not much", that's fine — some books are meant to be enjoyed and released. But seek out the ones that stick.

Those are worth reading slowly.
