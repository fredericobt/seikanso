A design system is a promise. It says: these are the decisions we've already made, so you don't have to make them again.

But promises require trust. And trust requires maintenance.

## The Documentation Trap

Most design systems die not from abandonment but from drift. The documentation says one thing. The product does another. Designers stop consulting the system because the system stopped being true.

## What Makes Systems Last

The systems that outlast their original authors share one thing: they're treated as living documents, not archives.

Someone is responsible. Decisions are revisited. Exceptions are explained, not hidden.

The goal is not consistency for its own sake — it's consistency in service of clarity. When the system earns trust, designers stop fighting it and start building with it.

That's when it becomes invisible. That's when it works.
